Introduction

Seldom Seen - The Artist Formely Known As Tosti

Since 2018 i changed my name to Seldom Seen because i didn't find the name Tosti not fitting anymore to the music i'm making.
The name Tosti rimes with Josti , which is a nickname i got given a long time ago. A Josti , in the netherlands , is a member of an ensemble of people who are mentally challenged called the Josti Band.
And a Tosti in dutch is a grilled cheese sandwich! So yes that name was fun for a while , but i figured i needed a less offensive and more mysterious name.
And so i renamed myself to Seldom Seen , because you don't often see a person like me , or whatever.. it's just a name! And it's not about ego , remember?!
Exact! , it's about the music!

Past Present Future

Seldom Seen - Biography

As I was born in 1983, I grew up listening to all kinds of music, but the music that really sticked was music that had some form of electronica. Especially the warm and analog sounds from synthesizers are sounds that I really love, such as from the Eurythmics, Steve Miller Band, Vangelis, Kraftwerk and Jean Michel Jarre, to name a few.
In the early 90's I started to listen to a much harder sound of electronics, namely house. Back in the day, I would go over to my best friend's house to tape all the new music that was on the radio or whatever my friend's older brother would have bought on vinyl or cd. My first record that I bought was Prodigy's "Everybody in the place". An epic record that I played over and over. I would also watch MTV's Partyzone on Friday night where I was mesmerized by all the video clips. Especially the clip from "The Future Sound Of London" blew my mind.
Later on in the 90's, I also started messing around with computers and found a way to make music. I was 12 when I started with Impulse Tracker, a music producing program that ran in DOS. For a couple of years I messed around with computers, but after a major crash on my harddrive I stopped making music for a while...
I was in my late teens as the 90's came to an end and I went in search of a more 'extreme' kind of house music. In my hometown Eindhoven, I would go to every drum 'n bass party I could find, like Redzone in de Effenaar or one of the techno nights organized by Zodiak Commune. The one party I remember particularly well was the epic queens day festival. And not to forget, all the squat parties I ended up in. In these squats, everything was a bit more raw, but also more pure, as was the music. Besides house, jungle and drum 'n bass, I fell in love with the acid techno and rave sounds.
After the turn of the century, I was still searching for that pure underground feeling. Somehow I always felt there was more to a rave then just the music. Turned out I was right. After a sweaty jungle night in cafe de Bakkerij, I was chilling out and having a chat with a guy and at a moment he reached in his pocket and took a piece of paper with some punky pictures on it and a cell phone number. It was a flyer for a freeparty, as they are called. He instructed me to call the number on the date of the party around 2200 and wait for instructions.
That Saturday evening, me and some friends called the number, followed the vague route descriptions and ended up in an old abandonded warehouse in the Breda area. There was a dodgy entrance going into the very narrow cellar underneath the warehouse. We couldn't see much because it was full of people, smoke from smoke machines, a couple of strobes and most importantly, very disorientating, pounding beats. These beats were something else, it was a lot of things: acid, hardcore, drum 'n bass, basically a lot of elements of house music. Some time later on, I found out this kind of music is called Tekno. The bastard brother of techno.
In 2004, I came in contact with more freeparty's, expanded my network and went on going to a lot of party's. And not only in the Netherlands, but also in France, Germany and Czech Republic. At one point, me and my friends decided to start organizing parties ourself. We bought some old equipment and soon enough we had created a soundsystem. To this day, we are still throwing parties! Since 2007, I picked up music production again. I found out it's better and more fun to use synthesizers and drummachines, then solely the computer. One machine that I really love is the korg electribe EMX1.
After 12+ years of knob twiddling, beat juggling and various performances, I am ready to present my music to the people who need and/or want it. For a long time I felt insecure about my music, but after some positive feedback and constructive criticism from the lovely people around me, I'm taking steps to start bringing out tracks. One good way of reaching a crowd is having an online presence. As you may notice, that is coming along nicely ;)
First of all, I want to release the tracks on the internet to see if people like it and I want to encourage them to give me some feedback. After some revising, I plan to bring it out on vinyl, so it can be played wherever, whenever.
So if you want to listen to my tracks, go ahead and visit the music section . If you feel like leaving me a message with your feedback or with anything you want to say, go to the contact form. And if you want to know if I'm playing somewhere anytime soon, check out the events . This concludes my biography!
C U soon even though I'm Seldom Seen ;) 1love
